Rio Claro Farmers Waxes Paulinia Robots to Move into Semifinals

Rio Claro Farmers pummeled Paulinia Robots 108.00 to 66.02, making it two wins in as many head-to-head matchups. They go to 10-4, while Paulinia Robots drops to 7-7. Rio Claro Farmers moves their historical playoff record against Paulinia Robots to 2-1. In the teams’ last postseason matchup in the semifinals in 2017, Rio Claro Farmers wasn’t as fortunate. Aaron Rodgers passed for three touchdowns and did the most damage this week, getting 30.90 points. The Green Bay gunslinger has turned out to be a solid sixth-round grab for Rio Claro Farmers and has played like a top-tier quarterback. On top of the loss, Paulinia Robots had a starter score zero points (DeVante Parker).

Conner had plenty of opportunities for Paulinia Robots, but could only manage 18 total yards on 10 touches.

Conversely, Rio Claro Farmers has a player trending the opposite way, as Mark Andrews has substantially underperformed his year-to-date average recently.

On the heels of a second-half surge, Rio Claro Farmers headed for the playoffs as the No. 3 seed. They move on to the semifinals where they will face No. 2-seeded Limeira Guara Wolf, who is coming off a bye week. These teams last met in Week 6, with Limeira Guara Wolf winning, 84.48 to 51.20. Paulinia Robots ended the regular season in sixth place with a record of 7-6. Better than their record indicated, they ranked first in the league in points per game with 123.28. Paulinia Robots was sent home quickly this postseason, losing in their first playoff matchup.
